Schoonmoeder

The task was to design a poster based on a joke. First you had to choose a joke and, with visual typography, you had to give a chosen context to the joke. It was aimed at learning how to work with typography on a big format as the one of a poster.

My poster was based on a joke about an architect that made a house for someone who wanted everything circular in his house. I wanted to give it the form of an architect plan, but also use circles. For the architect, I used a very classic typography to express his seriousness and for the other person, I put his words in circles and used Futura, a rounded typography. Some features around the text were added to make the poster look like an architect’s plan. By using only black, white and grey it creates a formal and architectural design. The title is also an important element in the poster and is placed in the corner on purpose, it is the conclusion of the joke but you understand it only if you have read the joke until the end.
