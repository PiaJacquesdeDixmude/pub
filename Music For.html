Music For...

The task was to make a cover for a vinyl record chosen from a given list. The aim was to make a total new design for a cover of an already existing music record and to make your own interpretation of the album. The labels had to be presented on the cover. We had work with the style of the artist, his music and in relation with the time when the album came out. The aim was to learn to collect iconographic elements on a already existing subject. It was also to give a visual identity to something as abstract as music.

I had chosen to work with the album “Music for falling from trees” from Peter Broderick. He wrote this music for a modern dance “falling from trees” piece show by the choreographer Adrienne Hart. The performance goes on the different phases that a mentally ill patient goes through. The music is very melancholic. I took this photo, and reworked it to show a painful process of mental sickness. I used the colour yellow inside the plate cover to show the inner madness of the person and red for the inner sleeve. It brings contrast with the yellow and express how a mentally ill person changes strongly of moods. At the back of the cover I reworked the photo of the golden rain-tree to highlight the importance of the yellow, the colour of mental illness and madness.
