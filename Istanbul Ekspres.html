Istanbul Ekspres

Istanbul Ekpres was the first assignment of second year. We had to create an identity for a film festival in Gent. The three posters are a part of the festival, and had to have the same visual identity. All the three were on the Istanbul Pogrom but the films were made at different times and exploited the subject from different point of views. The Istanbul pogrom were organized and extremely violent attacks directed primarily at Istanbul’s Greek minority in 1955. It comes from a long history of wars and migration movements.

The films are made from the point of view of the Greeks who were victims of these conflicts. To create a recognizable identity, I first used the colour red, a strong colour in terms of signification (conflict, blood pain but also love) and which is also the colour of the Turkish flag. The images come from the films and are all in black and white, to bring the attention the red of the typography. The different elements come back in each poster, always in the same form, with the title of the film in a circle form, like a stamp on a letter.

We can clearly see that the three posters are linked together but they can also be seen individually, each one has the required information for somebody who would like to see the film. I focussed especially on setting the typography and the images in a balance way as both are as important.
